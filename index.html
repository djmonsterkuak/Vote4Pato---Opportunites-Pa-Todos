Why This Matters

Opportunities are
harder to reach.

Not every student has a job or internship to gain technical skills. Not everyone knows a professor well enough to knock on their door. Not everyone knows where to start. That gap is real — and it is exactly what I am here to close.

01 — The Skills Gap
No job or internship?
Technical skills stay out of reach without hands-on experience. Students who are not employed early fall behind — not because of ability, but because of access.
02 — The Connection Gap
Don't know professors?
Research opportunities go unnoticed without those connections. Most students never reach out — not because they are uninterested, but because they do not know how or who to ask.
03 — The Roadmap Gap
No clear path forward?
Students do not know what workshops, tools, or opportunities exist for them. The information is scattered — I want to put it all in one place, accessible to everyone.
My Vision
"Every student deserves the tools, the knowledge, and the connections to thrive — regardless of their circumstances."

As Director of Academics and Research, I will build a bridge between students and the opportunities, professors, labs, and technical skills that will shape their futures. This is not just about holding events — it is about creating a lasting infrastructure that serves every student in this organization, at every stage of their journey.

The Platform

5 Core Initiatives

A concrete, student-first plan — built around the real gaps I see in our community.

01
LabLink
A curated, living directory of university research labs with open positions, professor bios, and application guidance — updated every semester.
02
Build It Workshops
Monthly hands-on STEAM sessions covering Arduino, CAD, coding, 3D printing, data science, and more. Not lectures — real, applied skills.
03
Faculty Coffee Chats
Informal 30-minute sessions connecting students with professors and researchers — lowering the barrier to meaningful academic relationships.
04
No Internship? No Problem.
Structured technical learning paths, peer teaching sessions, and a portfolio-building program for every student — regardless of employment status.
05
Miner Spark Research Course and Showcase
An end-of-semester symposium where students present and celebrate their research work — creating visibility, credibility, and a culture of academic achievement within SHPE and MAES.
Initiative 01

LabLink

A living directory of university research labs — updated every semester — with open positions, professor bios, and everything you need to walk through the door with confidence.

Searchable Lab Directory
Browse labs by department, topic, or skill required. No more guessing who does what.
Professor Spotlights
Short Q&A profiles so students know who to approach, what they research, and what they look for.
Open Positions Board
Real-time postings for research roles and volunteer spots — maintained and verified each semester.
Application Guidance
Email templates and step-by-step guidance for reaching out to professors for the first time.
Initiative 02

Build It Workshops

Hands-on STEAM, every month.

Not lectures. Not slideshows. Real sessions where you build, break, and learn technical skills that will follow you through your career.

Electronics
Arduino, circuit building, breadboard fundamentals
Coding
Python, web development, automation scripts, machine learning and AI
CAD & 3D Printing
SolidWorks, Fusion 360, prototyping workflows, and advanced 3D Printing
Data Science
Excel, Python, Minitab, Veusz, data visualization tools
Embedded Systems
Raspberry Pi, microcontrollers, sensors
Lab Skills
Lab safety, equipment use, scientific documentation
Initiative 04

No Internship?
No Problem.

You should not need a job to learn technical skills. This program gives every student a structured path to build real, demonstrable knowledge — starting from wherever they are right now.

A
Structured Learning Tracks
Guided weekly challenges organized by skill level — from beginner to advanced — in topics like programming, design, and engineering fundamentals.
B
Project Portfolio Builder
Build real, documented projects you can show to employers, research mentors, and graduate programs. Tangible proof of your skills.
C
Peer Teaching Sessions
Students who have a skill teach students who want to learn it. Community-driven, accessible, and practical — because the best way to reinforce a skill is to teach it.
Initiative 05

Research Showcase

An end-of-semester symposium where SHPE and MAES students present their research, projects, and academic work. A space to be seen, to inspire others, and to build a culture of scholarship within our community.

About the Candidate

Patricio Beltran

As an Aerospace Engineering student at UTEP and a member of the Sun City Summit Rocket Team, I am driven by a single mission: Opportunities Pa’ Todos. My technical foundation was built through research with NASA, AFRL, and the W.M. Keck Center, but my passion lies in mentorship. Having managed large-scale initiatives for over 200 students, I understand the logistics of leadership and the importance of trust. As your SHPE Research Mentor and advocate, I am here to ensure that no matter your starting point, the door to high-level research and technical mastery is open to you.

I’m running to be the bridge between you and the career you deserve. If someone ever told you "no," I’m here to help you get to "yes." Opportunities Pa’ Todos—because when one of us climbs, we all rise.
